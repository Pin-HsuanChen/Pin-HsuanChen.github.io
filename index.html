About me

I am Pin-Hsuan Chen, a Ph.D student in Institute of Human Factors and Human-System Interaction, the Department of Industrial Engineering at Tsinghua University. I am advised by Pei-Luen Patrick Rau.

My research interests lie in the intersection of human-computer interaction, human factors and neuroergonomics. Specifically, I focus on boredom problems while human workers are interacting with intelligent systems. In my research, I particularly study how boredom can impair cognitive performances and how to mitigate negative influences of boredom. To achieve my goals, I investigate cognitive functions and emotional response by physiological data (e.g., EEG).
